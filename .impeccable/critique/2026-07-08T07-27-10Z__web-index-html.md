#### Anti-Patterns Verdict

**LLM assessment**: This does not immediately read as AI-generated. It reads as a real operational PVR interface with a confident, restrained system UI. The strongest recent improvement is that the header, nav, metrics, and schedule states now have enough weight to feel intentional. The remaining issue is not decorative slop; it is operational density. Some controls are compressed below comfortable touch size, and the schedule mode hides too much global context.

**Deterministic scan**: `detect.mjs --json web/index.html web/styles.css web/app.js` returned `[]`. No detector findings for gradient text, side-stripe accents, ghost-card patterns, over-rounded cards, decorative stripe backgrounds, or other registered slop families.

**Visual overlays**: Browser DOM mutation succeeded, but loading `http://localhost:8400/detect.js` failed because the live-server process was not reachable after startup. No reliable user-visible overlay is available. Fallback signals used: CLI detector output, Playwright DOM inspection, desktop/mobile viewport measurements.

#### Overall Impression

The WUI is now credible as an operator dashboard. The TV-guide schedule is the right centerpiece, and the stronger chrome makes the app feel less tentative. The biggest opportunity is to make the schedule view safer and more self-explanatory under pressure: preserve critical system status, enlarge or regroup the smallest controls, and tame the action clusters.

#### What's Working

- The schedule grid has a concrete domain shape: time rail, sticky channel heads, category colors, and reserved/recording states create an immediately recognizable TV operations surface.
- The topbar/nav/metric hierarchy now gives the dashboard a strong first read. Counts are visible, the active nav is obvious, and the status badge is hard to miss on the dashboard.
- The design system is restrained and consistent enough for a product UI. It avoids decorative gradients, fake-glass panels, huge radii, and generic card-grid filler.

#### Priority Issues

**[P1] Schedule mode hides global operational status**

**Why it matters**: In `body.view-schedule`, the topbar and status badge are hidden. Operators are likely to stay in the schedule view, so "オペレータ停止中" disappears from the main working context. That is a high-stakes state for this product.

**Fix**: Keep a compact status capsule in the schedule nav, next to the filter controls. Include operator state plus recording count/reserved count. It should remain visible while scrolling the guide.

**Suggested command**: `$impeccable harden`

**[P1] Schedule controls and channel actions fall below reliable touch target size**

**Why it matters**: Browser inspection found many interactive elements at 26-28px high: schedule selects, hide/restore/clear buttons, channel-head links, and small "視聴"/"XSPF" actions. This is workable with a mouse, but poor for mobile, touch laptops, low-vision users, and stressful operator use.

**Fix**: Raise compact controls to at least 36px in dense desktop and 44px on mobile, or move low-frequency controls behind a menu while keeping primary filters large enough. Preserve density by grouping controls, not by shrinking hit areas.

**Suggested command**: `$impeccable adapt`

**[P1] Recorded-program action clusters are too flat**

**Why it matters**: A recorded row can expose many actions at the same visual level: M2TS, 視聴, 720p視聴, 低画質視聴, 詳細視聴, M2TS指定, XSPF, 保存, 静止画, 削除. Users must parse too many equally weighted options, and destructive actions sit in the same action band as routine playback/download actions.

**Fix**: Promote one primary action, group playback variants under a segmented/menu control, group export/download actions separately, and keep delete visually and spatially distinct.

**Suggested command**: `$impeccable distill`

**[P2] Filter consequences are not explained in-place**

**Why it matters**: Schedule filters are powerful, but the visible area does not clearly summarize "40 channels, 2 hidden, BS only, anime only, date X" in one sentence. Users must infer state from several controls.

**Fix**: Add a compact filter summary row or status text in schedule mode. Make reset/clear state explicit, especially after channel hiding.

**Suggested command**: `$impeccable clarify`

**[P2] Empty/error recovery is present but passive**

**Why it matters**: Empty states such as "録画中の番組はありません" are clear, but operational failure states need direct recovery paths. If logs/config/schedule fail, users need retry, diagnostics, or a next step.

**Fix**: Pair important error and empty states with a concrete action: retry load, open logs, open settings, or jump to rules depending on context.

**Suggested command**: `$impeccable harden`

#### Persona Red Flags

**Alex (Power User)**: Alex can move fast because the app exposes direct actions and dense lists, but the action clusters are not optimized. Playback/export/delete actions require reading many same-weight buttons, and no visible keyboard shortcuts or command path exist for common operator tasks.

**Sam (Accessibility-Dependent User)**: Sam benefits from visible focus styling and labeled nav, but many controls are smaller than comfortable touch/focus targets. Schedule states also lean heavily on color/category tint; reserved/recording labels help, but more non-color state summaries would reduce risk.

**Casey (Distracted Mobile User)**: Casey gets a responsive nav with no horizontal page overflow, but schedule mode has a horizontal control strip full of 28px controls. Important state is hidden above the schedule, and several actions are too small or too close together for one-handed use.

#### Minor Observations

- `--console` topbar creates a strong operator mood, but schedule mode loses that reassurance entirely.
- Category colors are useful, but long Japanese titles inside 24px-high schedule cards quickly become scan noise.
- The dashboard's "放送中" list can become extremely tall and visually dominate the actual recording/reserve summary.
- Form controls have a coherent shape, but small buttons use the same rectangular vocabulary as primary buttons, making hierarchy rely mostly on size.

#### Questions to Consider

- Should schedule mode be treated as the primary cockpit, with persistent operator health always visible?
- Which recorded-row action is the true default: play, download, details, or convert?
- Are channel hide/restore controls frequent enough to deserve first-row placement, or should they live behind a channel tools menu?
- What is the one state an operator must never miss: recording failure, operator stopped, conflict, or next reservation?
